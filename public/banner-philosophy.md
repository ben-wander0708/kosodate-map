# 萌芽の静けさ（Seedling Stillness）

A design philosophy born from the tension between rootedness and movement — the moment just before a seed cracks open, charged with potential yet visually serene.

## Form and Space

Space is not emptiness but intention. Every margin breathes deliberately. Vertical rhythm is paramount: elements stack with the quiet discipline of Japanese woodblock printing, each zone owning its territory completely. Nothing bleeds into the next. The canvas is a series of considered silences punctuated by precisely placed voices.

## Color and Material

The palette draws from a single living source — deep forest green, the color of moss after rain, of new leaves held to light. This green does not shout; it anchors. Against pure white it becomes architectural, structural. A thin border frames the entire composition like the mounting of a scroll — containment as care, boundary as gift. The green header is a dense canopy, the white body is the clearing beneath.

## Scale and Rhythm

Typography moves from large to impossibly small — a cascade of scale that creates visual depth on a flat plane. The headline breathes at mid-scale, restrained. The body text is fine-grain, clinical, each item a whisper. The call-to-action pill returns to weight and warmth: a closing gesture, an invitation. This rhythm — strong, quiet, strong — mirrors the exhale-hold-exhale of intentional breath.

## Composition and Visual Hierarchy

Information does not explain itself; it presents itself. Each functional element — map, checklist, sharing, access — is rendered as a mark, not a paragraph. The eye travels downward through a deliberately designed sequence: identity → promise → proof → action. This sequence is meticulous, the product of deep expertise in how attention actually moves through constrained vertical space.

## Craftsmanship Imperative

This work must appear as though it took countless hours. Every pixel of padding measured. Every font size chosen with painstaking deliberation. The alignment of checkmarks to text, the radius of the pill button, the weight of the border stroke — each decision the mark of someone operating at the absolute top of their craft. The final artifact should feel inevitable, as if no other arrangement could ever have been correct.
